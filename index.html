GSAT™ Methodology

GSAT™ (Governance Stress Assessment & Transmission) evaluates governance performance under forward-looking stress conditions across five domains: escalation, capital integration, dependency exposure, product governance, and decision authority.

Governance performance is translated into structured outputs including GSAT score, Governance Resilience Rating (GRR), and Transmission Risk Modifier (TRM), enabling supervisory-grade assessment of governance stability under stress.

Governance Stress Testing as a System
HSCS applies GSAT™ to measure governance performance under stress—translating complex structures into supervisory-grade outputs, actionable findings, and continuous monitoring.
GSAT™ Simulation
An interactive simulation of governance performance under stress conditions. Users adjust structural inputs to generate GSAT scores, Governance Resilience Ratings (GRR), and Transmission Risk Modifiers (TRM).
Governance Stress Test
A structured evaluation of governance performance under forward-looking stress conditions across five GSAT domains, delivered as a formal governance stress test report.
Continuous Governance Monitoring
A recurring application of GSAT™ to monitor governance resilience over time, detect structural drift, and reassess performance under evolving conditions.
When to Use Governance Stress Testing
Platform Transition
Assess governance resilience during system migrations, clearing changes, and infrastructure transformation.
Structural Change
Evaluate governance across entities, jurisdictions, and operating model redesign.
Board Oversight
Establish governance as a measurable discipline through recurring stress testing and monitoring.
Engagement Model

HSCS delivers governance stress testing through a structured assessment process aligned with supervisory expectations.

Engagements typically include data collection, governance diagnostic, stress simulation, and delivery of a formal Governance Stress Test Report with GSAT score, GRR classification, and targeted realignment actions.

Continuous monitoring can be established through periodic reassessment and scenario-based governance testing.

Assessment Scope

Assessment scope is determined based on institutional complexity, number of entities, asset structure, and transition conditions.

Engagements are tailored to reflect governance architecture, regulatory environment, and operational dependencies.
